What We Look For in a Wiper Blade

We evaluate every wiper on five criteria:

  • Rain clearing: How cleanly does it wipe in moderate and heavy rain? Any streaking, chattering, or missed spots?
  • Durability: How long before performance degrades? We test at 3, 6, 9, and 12 months.
  • Noise: Does it squeak, chatter, or create wind noise at highway speeds?
  • Snow/ice handling: Can it clear slush? Does ice build up on the blade or arm?
  • Installation: How easy is it to install? Does it fit the most common arm types without adapters?

Beam vs Conventional vs Hybrid

Quick summary (we have a full comparison article):

  • Beam (bracketless): Modern design, conforms to curved glass, fewer clogging points. Best for most drivers. All our top 3 picks are beam blades.
  • Conventional (bracket): Old-school metal frame with multiple contact points. Cheaper, but they collect ice, snow, and debris in the brackets. Only buy if your car specifically requires them.
  • Hybrid: Aerodynamic shell over a conventional frame. Middle ground. The Michelin Stealth Ultra is the best example.

How Often to Replace Wipers

Most wipers last 6-12 months before performance drops noticeably. Silicone blades (PIAA, Rain-X AdvantEdge) can last 12-18 months. Signs you need new wipers: streaking, chattering, skipping across the glass, or visible cracks/tears in the rubber. Don’t wait for a downpour to find out your wipers are shot—check them before rainy season and before winter.
