Best Rear Windshield Wipers

April 2026 · 6 min read

Rear wipers are the most neglected part of most vehicles. People replace their front wipers regularly and completely forget the rear exists—until they can’t see out of the back window in a rainstorm. If your rear wiper is leaving streaks or barely clearing water, it’s time. Here’s what to buy.

The Challenge with Rear Wipers

Rear wipers are different from front wipers in a few important ways:

  • Non-standard connections: Unlike front wipers that mostly use J-hook arms, rear wiper arms vary wildly between manufacturers. Many use proprietary clip or pin connections.
  • Smaller sizes: Rear wipers typically range from 10" to 16", much smaller than fronts.
  • More sun exposure: Rear glass gets constant UV exposure, which degrades rubber faster. The blade is also exposed to more direct sunlight than front wipers tucked under the hood.

When to Replace Your Rear Wiper

If it streaks, chatters, or leaves a film, replace it. Most rear wipers degrade faster than front wipers because they’re exposed to more UV light and rarely get used (so the rubber dries out). A good rule: replace your rear wiper every time you replace your front wipers. At $10-14 per blade, there’s no reason to skip it.
